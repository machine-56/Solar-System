Mars

Mars is often called Earth’s little brother because of its similarities in geography and past water activity. Evidence suggests that Mars once had rivers, lakes, and possibly an ocean, but its atmosphere thinned over time, making it a cold desert. The planet experiences gigantic dust storms that can engulf the entire planet. Its two small moons, Phobos and Deimos, are thought to be captured asteroids. Scientists are searching for microbial life, and future Mars missions aim for human colonization.
