The Sun

The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star (G2V), commonly referred to as a yellow dwarf, though its light is actually white. It generates energy through nuclear fusion, converting hydrogen into helium at its core, releasing immense heat and light. Its surface temperature reaches about 5,500°C (9,932°F), while the core burns at 15 million°C. The Sun's magnetic field drives solar activity, including sunspots, solar flares, and coronal mass ejections, which can impact Earth's atmosphere and technology.
