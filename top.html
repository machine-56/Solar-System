Top 10 Biggest moon...

Ganymede (Jupiter)
  • Diameter: 5,268 km
  • Mass: 1.48 × 10²³ kg
  • Largest moon in the Solar System, even bigger than Mercury!
  • Has a magnetic field and possibly an underground ocean.
Titan (Saturn)
  • Diameter: 5,151 km
  • Mass: 1.35 × 10²³ kg
  • Only moon with a dense atmosphere, mainly nitrogen.
  • Has seas of liquid methane and ethane.
Callisto (Jupiter)
  • Diameter: 4,821 km
  • Mass: 1.08 × 10²³ kg
  • One of the most heavily cratered objects in the Solar System.
  • Might have a subsurface ocean beneath its icy crust.
Io (Jupiter)
  • Diameter: 3,643 km
  • Mass: 8.93 × 10²² kg
  • Most volcanically active body in the Solar System.
  • Has over 400 active volcanoes, powered by Jupiter’s tidal forces.
Moon (Earth)
  • Diameter: 3,474 km
  • Mass: 7.35 × 10²² kg
  • The only natural satellite of Earth.
  • Influences tides and Earth's axial stability.
Europa (Jupiter)
  • Diameter: 3,122 km
  • Mass: 4.80 × 10²² kg
  • Has a global subsurface ocean beneath its icy shell.
  • A strong candidate for extraterrestrial life.
Triton (Neptune)
  • Diameter: 2,710 km
  • Mass: 2.14 × 10²² kg
  • Only large moon with a retrograde orbit.
  • Has cryovolcanoes that eject nitrogen gas.
Titania (Uranus)
  • Diameter: 1,578 km
  • Mass: 3.53 × 10²¹ kg
  • Largest moon of Uranus.
  • Likely has faults and canyons, indicating geological activity.
Rhea (Saturn)
  • Diameter: 1,527 km
  • Mass: 2.31 × 10²¹ kg
  • Second-largest moon of Saturn.
  • May have a thin atmosphere of oxygen and carbon dioxide.
Oberon (Uranus)
  • Diameter: 1,523 km
  • Mass: 3.03 × 10²¹ kg
  • Second-largest moon of Uranus.
  • Surface covered with craters and deep faults.
