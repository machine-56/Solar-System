Neptune

Neptune is an ice giant, composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane, giving it its deep blue color. It has the fastest winds in the solar system, sometimes exceeding 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph). The planet has faint rings and 14 known moons, the largest being Triton, which orbits backward, suggesting it was a captured object. The Voyager 2 spacecraft provided most of our knowledge of Neptune, revealing its dynamic storms, including the Great Dark Spot, which later disappeared.
