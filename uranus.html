Uranus

Uranus is unique because its axial tilt is 98°, making it appear as if it’s rolling along its orbit. It’s the coldest planet, with temperatures dropping to -224°C (-371°F). Its faint rings and 27 known moons add to its mystique. The planet’s blue-green color comes from methane absorbing red light. A day on Uranus lasts about 17 hours, while its orbit around the Sun takes 84 Earth years. The planet's strong winds, reaching 900 km/h, contribute to its dynamic atmosphere.
