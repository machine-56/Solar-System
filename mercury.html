Mercury

Mercury is a rocky, heavily cratered world, resembling the Moon in appearance. Due to its lack of atmosphere, it cannot retain heat, causing the biggest temperature difference in the solar system. Despite its proximity to the Sun, Mercury isn't the hottest planet—Venus holds that title. A day on Mercury is 176 Earth days long, meaning its surface experiences prolonged periods of scorching heat and deep freeze. The planet has a high iron content, making it extremely dense. NASA's MESSENGER spacecraft provided most of our detailed knowledge of Mercury.
