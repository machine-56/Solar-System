Jupiter

Jupiter is a gas giant made mostly of hydrogen and helium, lacking a solid surface. Its Great Red Spot, a massive anticyclonic storm, is twice the size of Earth. Jupiter has the strongest magnetic field of all planets, trapping radiation and creating intense auroras. The planet’s moon system includes Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system, Europa, which may harbor a subsurface ocean, and Io, the most volcanically active body known. Jupiter plays a crucial role in protecting Earth by capturing or deflecting asteroids with its gravity.
