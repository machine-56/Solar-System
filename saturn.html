Saturn

Saturn’s iconic rings are made of countless ice and rock particles, some as small as dust grains, others as large as mountains. The planet’s low density means it would float if placed in a giant ocean. It has more than 140 moons, including Titan, which has lakes of liquid methane and a thick atmosphere. Saturn’s hexagonal polar storm is a bizarre feature observed at its north pole. Like Jupiter, Saturn is mostly composed of hydrogen and helium and has an extremely fast rotation, causing it to flatten at the poles.
